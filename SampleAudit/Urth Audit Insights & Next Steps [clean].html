Strategy Roadmap  
In our upcoming roadmap, our goal is to implement new automations and enhance personalisation.  
We also plan to expand your existing ows by adding more touchpoints. This strategy aims to  
maximise email activity revenue and ensure effective engagement with customers at different  
stages of their journey through tailored messaging.  
We'll explore the following additional automations aside from optimising your existing ows:  
Cross-Selling: There's an opportunity to implement dedicated cross-sell ows, customised  
according to the number of orders and collections customers have purchased. These ows could  
include dedicated rst, second, and third purchase sequences, utilising segmentation based on  
the specic collections customers have ordered from.  
Loyalty Program Flows: In line with the objective to promote your loyalty program, we plan to  
create new loyalty program ows. Specically, we are considering a loyalty welcome ow for new  
program members and a loyalty points reminder ow.  
Add To Cart: While auditing your ows, we noticed the absence of an Abandoned Cart ow  
triggered by 'Added To Cart.' Currently, there's an Abandoned Cart ow triggered by 'Started  
Checkout,' which differs from the 'Added To Cart' trigger.  
The key distinction between these triggers lies in the customer's journey: 'Added To Cart' captures  
users at an earlier stage when they express initial interest by adding items to their cart, while 'Started  
Checkout' indicates a more advanced stage, signifying higher intent as they move closer to  
completing a purchase.  
Having separate ows for 'Started Checkout' and 'Added To Cart' triggers is benecial because  
customers at these stages are on different paths in their buying journey. Those who've initiated  
checkout are usually more committed and interested in completing a purchase compared to those  
who've only added items to their cart.  
By setting up distinct ows, we can tailor our communication more precisely: the 'Started Checkout'  
ow can focus on incentivizing those who are closer to purchase, while the 'Added To Cart' ow can  
gently nudge and re-engage users who are still exploring options. This strategic approach optimises  
the chances of recovering potentially lost sales at different stages of the customer journey.  
Customer Milestone Flows: Establishing a customer milestone ow for Urth, such as a Birthday  
Flow and First Purchase Anniversary Flow, is particularly valuable based on insights from the  
cohort report for retention in Shopify.  
The report highlights a trend where, in 2023, rst-time customers show low retention rates, with a  
diminishing percentage returning for subsequent purchases as time passes. The highest rate, ranging  
from 4-6% of the customers, is observed within the rst 0-1 months after the initial purchase, but this  
diminishes over the next months.  
In response to these ndings, activating a Birthday Flow provides an opportunity to nudge  
customers and offer them another incentive to make a purchase on their special day. Similarly,  
implementing a First Purchase Anniversary Flow serves to encourage them to consider another  
purchase around the 12th month since their initial transaction, strategically engaging customers at  
key points and increasing the chances of reactivation and fostering longer-term customer loyalty.
